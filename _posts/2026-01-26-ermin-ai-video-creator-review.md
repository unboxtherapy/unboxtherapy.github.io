This is arguably the flagship feature, and it's where AI Video Creator truly aims to shine. The sales page highlights "advanced automation features" designed to streamline the entire video production process. In my testing, I found this primarily manifests in several key areas:

*   **Script-to-Video Generation:** You input a text script or even just a few bullet points, and the AI analyzes the content, breaking it down into scenes. It then intelligently selects relevant video clips, images, and background music from its internal library to match the narrative. For example, if your script talks about "growing your business," the AI might automatically pull stock footage of thriving offices, people collaborating, or graphs showing upward trends.

## How Does AI Video Creator Work?

Understanding the workflow is key to appreciating the "user-friendly" claim. From my extensive testing, AI Video Creator follows a remarkably straightforward, almost wizard-like process, which is ideal for its target audience. Let me walk you through the typical steps:

### Step-by-Step Workflow Explanation

1.  **Login and Initiate Project:** You start by logging into your cloud-based dashboard. The main option you'll see prominently displayed is "Create New Video." Clicking this usually gives you a choice: start from scratch, use a template, or import an existing script. For most users, especially beginners, starting with a template is the fastest way to get going.

2.  **Input Content (Script/Topic):** This is where the AI begins its magic.
    *   **Text Input:** You'll paste your video script directly into a text box. The AI is designed to analyze this text for keywords, sentiment, and scene breaks.
    *   **Topic Input:** Alternatively, some automation modes allow you to simply input a topic or a few keywords, and the AI will attempt to generate a basic script or outline for you, which you can then refine. I found this particularly useful for brainstorming ideas or for very short social media snippets.

3.  **Select Style/Template:** If you didn't start with a template, this is where you'd choose a visual style. The platform typically offers various themes like "Corporate," "Explainer," "Promotional," "Minimalist," "Energetic," etc. These templates pre-define fonts, color schemes, transition types, and often suggest background music genres.

4.  **AI Generation & Media Selection:** Once your script and style are set, you hit the "Generate" button. The AI then:
    *   Breaks your script into individual scenes.
    *   Searches its internal stock media library (videos, images) for visuals that match the context of each scene's text. For instance, if your script mentions "boost sales," it might pull up clips of cash registers, happy customers, or growth charts.
    *   Synthesizes a voiceover from your text using your chosen AI voice.
    *   Selects appropriate background music based on the chosen style.
    *   Automatically applies transitions between scenes.

5.  **Review and Customize:** After the AI generates the initial draft (which usually takes a few minutes, depending on video length), you're presented with a timeline editor.
    *   **Scene-by-Scene Editing:** You can go through each scene, previewing the video. If a visual isn't quite right, you can easily swap it out for another suggestion from the library or upload your own media.
    *   **Text Overlays:** Adjust text, change fonts, colors, and positioning.
    *   **Voiceover & Music:** Tweak voiceover speed, volume, or select a different AI voice. Change background music or upload your own audio track.
    *   **Timing:** Adjust the duration of scenes to sync better with the voiceover
