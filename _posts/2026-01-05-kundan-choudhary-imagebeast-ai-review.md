### Iterate and Refine

Your first attempt might not be perfect, and that's normal. Use the first generation as a starting point, then adjust your description based on what you see. This iterative process helps you learn how the AI interprets different words and phrases.

### Study Examples

If ImageBeast AI provides example prompts or a gallery of user-created images, study these to understand what kinds of descriptions produce great results. Learning from others' successful prompts can accelerate your own learning curve.

### Keep a Prompt Library

When you discover text descriptions that work particularly well, save them in a document. Building a personal library of effective prompts means you don't have to reinvent the wheel each time you need similar images.

### Understand AI Limitations

Recognize that AI might struggle with very complex scenes involving many specific elements, unusual perspectives, or highly technical subjects. Having realistic expectations helps prevent frustration.

### Focus on Composition

Think about what makes a good image beyond just the subject. Consider elements like lighting, perspective, color scheme, and mood when writing your prompts. Describing these aspects can dramatically improve results.
